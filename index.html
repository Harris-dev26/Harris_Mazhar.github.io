Projects / المشاريع

Sanctions, Transitional Governments, and News Outlets: A Case Analysis on Syria

May 2025 - Ongoing

Key Technologies: Sentiment Analysis (DistilBERT), Tone Analysis (DeBERTa), Topic Modeling (TF-IDF, DBSCAN, TopicBERT), Named Entity Recognition (spaCy), Webscraping (Beautiful Soup)

  • This ongoing research analyzes narrative shifts in Syrian media by examining over 800 articles from key pro- and anti-transitional news outlets, employing advanced NLP techniques like sentiment, tone, and topic modeling to dissect discourse surrounding policy changes.
  • Initial findings indicate a significant increase in assertive tone among pro-transitional media following sanctions relief, while anti-transitional outlets, despite some nuanced shifts, have largely maintained their distinct oppositional perspectives.
  • The study currently suggests that international policy interventions have selective and alignment-mediated impacts on media narratives, particularly within highly polarized and transitional political contexts.
